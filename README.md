- Quoting Kant: ["Human exists for themselves"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Categorical_imperative#Second_formulation:_Humanity), **I believe no matter how advanced techonology/software becomes, it ultimately shall serve back to human's deepest and most primitive desires - their _own_ happiness**.
- Softwares, whether being advanced by contemporary AI or some "XYZ" in the next 100 years, MUST serve every **individual**'s wellness with values **directory** and **exclusively**, rather than serving as a tool by some organization/agencies to bring "values" to other organizations
- _Open Source_ is by far the most powerful tool that makes such self-autonomous advancement for **individual** possible.

I'm interested in 5 topics of tech world:

1. [Tech Management](https://qubitpi.github.io/management-blogs/) (I'm still playing with LeetCode (see right) today as part of **designing a more scientific hiring process for my team**)
2. [NLP](https://github.com/QubitPi#machine-learning-) Machine Learning

4. [React](https://github.com/QubitPi#frontend--)-based Frontend Dev with little more focus on the UI design
5. No-Spring [Webservice](https://github.com/QubitPi#backend-) Dev

I used to study technologies by [blogging](https://qubitpi.github.io/management-blogs/) extensively. Since I'm running a company, my obligations right now are more on doing well for _my employees_ and for _my company_, which leave me literally no time for the blogging.
